About this Edition

This is the definitive online edition of Encyclopedia Britannica's legendary 11th edition, Jorge Luis Borges's favorite reference book, etc. It is the 11th that T.S. Eliot's "Soul curled up on the window seat reading the Encyclopædia Britannica" is curled up with. 

For sheer reading pleasure the 11th is unsurpassed. Its contributors include such luminaries as Matthew Arnold, Bertrand Russell, J.B. Bury, George Saintsbury, Edmund Gosse, and many more. They were largely but by no means exclusively academics in an age when the term stood in for real scholarship and learning. Many display surprising expertise. Alfred North Whitehead co-authored GEOMETRY, as one might expect, but also contributed COTTON MATHER and SAINT GEORGE, as one might not. The best arguments for the 11th are inadvertently supplied, as so often, by its detractors. 

This version of the 11th enhances the original in several ways. It can be searched by text across the entire corpus and within article. The long articles -- and some, like ENGLISH HISTORY, are very long indeed -- begin with section links to facilitate internal navigation.

Although by the 11th edition Britannica was, as befits its name, primarily a British operation, it betrays its Scottish origins in several amusing ways, such as the immensely detailed entry on BAG-PIPES. It also had substantial contributions from many Americans, not least the financial one from Horace Everett Cooper, Britannica's publisher at the time. Hugh Chisholm gives a thorough account of the Encyclopaedia's history in his Editorial Preface and I will not repeat it here. 

The scholarly apparatus is extensive. There are 1,403 named authors in the 11th, and the Contributors index gives the credentials, biographical entries, and articles written for each. Each article includes the author's full name, linked to his entry; the print edition displayed only initials, which a curious user was obliged to look up. Volume and page of the original are displayed in the left margin for ease of citation. Shoulder-sections are displayed at the right. When an article is based on source that has not been proofread or contains potential OCR transcription errors, this is also marked. Articles can be searched by title, and there is also full-text search for articles as well as the entire corpus.
