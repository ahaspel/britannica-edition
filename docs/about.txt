About this Edition

Britannica's 11th edition is the best Encyclopedia ever written, and one can see why, as so often, by attending to its detractors.

This version of the 11th surpasses the original in several ways. It can be searched by text across the entire corpus and within article. The long articles -- and some, like ENGLISH HISTORY, are very long indeed -- begin with section links to facilitate internal navigation.

The scholarly apparatus is extensive. There is a complete and searchable Contributors index, where the original had one per volume, with no way to see all the articles written by a particular author. Articles include the contributor's name, linked to his entry, which shows his credentials as well as his other contributions. Volume and page of the original is shown in the left margin of each article for ease of citation.
